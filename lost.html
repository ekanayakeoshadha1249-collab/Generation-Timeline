The Lost Generation

1883–1900

The Lost Generation came of age during the chaos of World War I. They witnessed the collapse of empires, global conflict, and cultural disillusionment. Many sought meaning in art, literature, and travel, rebelling against traditional morals and nationalism. Authors like Ernest Hemingway and F. Scott Fitzgerald captured their sense of alienation and yearning for identity in a changing world.
